The Smoke That Thunders

Welcome to Victoria Falls — one of the greatest natural spectacles on Earth. Known locally as Mosi-oa-Tunya, “The Smoke That Thunders,” this UNESCO World Heritage Site is where the mighty Zambezi River crashes into a dramatic basalt gorge, releasing a roar that echoes for kilometers and a mist that rises like rain from the sky.

By day, rainbows shimmer endlessly in the spray. By night, during a full moon, the falls reveal one of nature’s rarest miracles — the magical Moonbow, a silver arc dancing in the darkness.
