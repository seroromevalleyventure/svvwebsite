Khami Ruins — Ancient Trade and Architecture

After the decline of Great Zimbabwe, Khami became the capital of the Torwa dynasty in the 15th century. Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Khami stands as a testament to advanced dry-stone architecture and centuries of trade with far-reaching civilizations, including China and Europe.

Highlights

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Second largest ancient stone monument after Great Zimbabwe
  • Former capital of the Torwa dynasty (16th century)
  • Artifacts show trade with China and Europe
  • Unique terraced dry-stone architecture
  • Rich archaeological and cultural significance
