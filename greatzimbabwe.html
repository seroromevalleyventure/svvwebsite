The Ancient Kingdom of Great Zimbabwe

Step back in time to the heart of Southern Africa’s ancient civilization. The Great Zimbabwe Ruins are the largest ancient stone structures south of the Sahara, built entirely without mortar between the 11th and 15th centuries. Once the royal capital of the Kingdom of Zimbabwe, this UNESCO World Heritage Site reveals a legacy of trade, power, and cultural sophistication.

Chapel of Saint Francis of Assisi

Masvingo • Half Day • Up to 8 Guests

Nestled near the Great Zimbabwe ruins, the St. Francis of Assisi Chapel stands as a peaceful spiritual landmark. Built during the colonial era, this humble chapel represents Zimbabwe’s religious and cultural evolution, offering travelers a serene space for reflection amidst the region’s ancient heritage.
